The Nth Term Test


Purple dots are the divergent series \(ln(n)\), orange dots are the convergent series \( \dfrac{1}{n^2} \). The x's are their respective sums.

Use this to check if a simple series diverges by evaluting the limit of \( a_n \) as \( n \) approaches infity. If the nth term is anything other than zero, the series diverges. This test is only capable of checking for divergence and cannot prove convergence.

\( \boxed{lim_{n\to \infty} a_n \ne 0 \implies \begin{array}{c} \infty \\ \sum \\ n=0 \end{array} a_n \text{diverges}} \)
