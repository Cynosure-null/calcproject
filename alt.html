The Alternating Series Test


Purple dots are the divergent series \( (-1)^n(n) \), orange dots are the convergent series \( (-1)^n(n^{-1}) \). The x's are their respective sums.

This test should be used when the series alternates between positive and negative. This occurs when the term \( (-1)^n \) is present.
Steps:
  1. remove the term \((-1)^n \) from the series definiton
  2. \( ( |a_{n+1}| < |a_n| \wedge lim_{n\to \infty}a_n=0 ) \implies \text{convergence} \)
  3. Otherwise, the series diverges
