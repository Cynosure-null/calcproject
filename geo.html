The Geometric Series Test


Purple dots are the divergent series \( 1.1^n\), orange dots are the convergent series \( (\dfrac{1}{3})^n \). The x's are their respective sums.

Use this test if a series matches the form \( a_n = ar^{n} \). if \( |r| < 1 \), the series converges. If else, it diverges. This test provides a simple way to get the sum of the series
\( \boxed{ \begin{array}{c} \infty \\ \sum \\ n=0 \end{array} ar^n = \dfrac{a}{1-r} \iff |r|<1} \)
