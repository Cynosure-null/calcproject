Absolute and Conditional Convergence


Purple dots are the conditionaly convergent series \( \frac{\left(-1\right)^{n}}{n} \), orange dots are the absoultuley convergent series \( \frac{\left(-1\right)^{n}}{n^{2}} \). The x's are their respective sums.

A series is absoultuley convergent when the sum of the absolute value of the series converges (\( \begin{array}{c} \infty \\ \sum \\ n=0 \end{array} |a_n|\)), and a series is conditionaly convergent when the value converges, but the absoulte value does not. Whenever a series is absoultuley convergent, it is also conditionaly convergent.
