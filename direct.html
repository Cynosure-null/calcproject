The Direct Comparison Test


Purple dots are the divergent series \( n^{-1}\), orange dots are the divergent series \( 2n^{-1} \). The x's are their respective sums.

This test proves that if all values of \( a_n \) is larger than all values of \( b_n \) and \(b_n\) is divergent, \( a_n \) is divergent.
\( \boxed{a_n \leq b_n \wedge a_n \text{ diverges} \implies \begin{array}{c} \infty \\ \sum \\ n=0 \end{array} b_n \text{ diverges} } \) \( \boxed{a_n \geq b_n \wedge a_n \text{ converges} \implies \begin{array}{c} \infty \\ \sum \\ n=0 \end{array} b_n \text{ converges} } \)
